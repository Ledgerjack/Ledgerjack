# Licence decision — RESOLVED: AGPL-3.0

The project is licensed under the **GNU Affero General Public License v3.0
(AGPL-3.0-or-later)**.

## Why AGPL-3.0
- Free for anyone to use and modify.
- Strong copyleft: anyone distributing it, OR running a modified version as a
  network service, must publish their source under the same licence.
- Stops LedgerJack being closed-sourced and sold as a proprietary product —
  which fits its free-and-private mission.
- The copyright holder keeps full ownership and may ALSO license it commercially
  on separate terms (dual licensing). The AGPL binds everyone else, not the owner.

## Two things to finish before publishing (both one-liners)
1. In `LICENSE`, replace `[COPYRIGHT HOLDER ...]` with your name or organisation.
2. Add the full official AGPL-3.0 legal text to `LICENSE`:
   - easiest: on GitHub use "Add file -> Choose a license template -> GNU AGPL
     v3.0" (it inserts the exact text), or
   - paste it from https://www.gnu.org/licenses/agpl-3.0.txt
   (The verbatim ~34 KB text wasn't auto-generated because it must be the exact
   official version and this build environment has no internet to verify it.)

## Optional but recommended
- Add a short SPDX header to source files:
  `// SPDX-License-Identifier: AGPL-3.0-or-later`
- If you plan to accept outside contributions and keep the option to dual-license,
  consider a lightweight Contributor Licence Agreement (CLA) or a DCO sign-off.
